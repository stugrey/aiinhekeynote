# Artsy Gallery Insights 2022 Report — Design Analysis

## Overview
An annual industry research report from Artsy, the online art marketplace, surveying 1,300+ galleries across 84 countries on business operations, digital sales, and collector trends. 27 slides. The vibe is gallery-wall-meets-data-report — it uses bold color blocking inspired by contemporary art to make survey data feel like an exhibition. Confident, institutional, visually striking.

## Color Palette
- **Light Blue / Sky** (~#A8D8EA) — the primary brand-adjacent color. Used as a full background on the TOC page and as a recurring block color on section dividers and data slides.
- **Coral / Salmon Red** (~#E06B6B) — warm, prominent on section dividers and as a large block on the cover grid.
- **Peach / Nude** (~#E8C8A0) — a warm, sandy neutral used as a large block color, especially on data visualization slides (e.g., the bar chart background on slide 9).
- **Lime Green / Chartreuse** (~#C8E050) — bright, acidic green used as a block on data slides and section dividers. Adds energy.
- **Pink / Orchid** (~#D98DC0) — soft pink/mauve, used as block backgrounds on key findings slides (slide 7) and cover grid.
- **Pale Yellow** (~#F0E8A0) — soft, warm yellow appearing in the color grid compositions.
- **Gold / Amber** (~#D8A848) — warm ochre/mustard, used as a bar color in charts and block fills.
- **Blue / Cobalt** (~#4060D0) — a deeper, more saturated blue used at edges of the cover grid. Provides depth against the lighter sky blue.
- **White** (#FFFFFF) — background for text-heavy content slides. Clean and neutral.
- **Black** (#000000) — all text. No colored text anywhere — black only, always.

The palette is remarkably broad — 7-8 distinct hues — but they're all at a similar medium saturation and brightness, giving them the feel of a curated gallery wall or a set of pigment swatches. No color dominates; they rotate and recombine.

## Typography
- **Primary Typeface**: A clean, modern sans-serif throughout (appears to be a geometric grotesque, similar to Unica77 or Artsy's custom face). Medium weight, highly legible.
- **Headings**: Very large, bold sans-serif. "Key Findings", "2022 Findings" — massive scale, left-aligned, black on color backgrounds. The type is the focal point.
- **Finding titles**: Large, bold, left-aligned statements that read as newspaper headlines — "Online Sales Continue to Grow", "Galleries still prefer bank and wire transfers over credit cards." These are designed to be scannable and quotable.
- **Numbered findings**: Large numerals (1, 2, 3, 4) left-aligned as anchors, with the finding title next to them in bold. Creates a clear, numbered hierarchy.
- **Body text**: Small, regular weight, set in narrow columns (2-3 columns on data pages). Dense but readable.
- **Navigation/breadcrumbs**: Tiny text top-left ("Artsy Gallery Insights: 2022 Report") and section label ("Key Findings", "2022 Findings") — consistent wayfinding on every slide.
- **Page numbers**: Top-right, small — print convention.

## Layout Patterns
- **Color-block grid as identity**: The cover (slide 1) and section dividers (slides 5, 8) use a grid of rectangular color blocks — different sizes, different hues — arranged in an asymmetric mosaic with white gaps between them. This is the signature visual device. It evokes a gallery wall of colored canvases or a Mondrian-inspired composition.
- **Two-register content slides**: Finding pages (slides 6-7) split into a top half and bottom half, each with a different background color. Finding 1-2 on lime/yellow (slide 6), finding 3-4 on pink/mauve (slide 7). The color change signals a new finding without needing a new slide.
- **Left-heavy text, right-side data**: On the data-rich slide 9, the left ~40% is body text in columns, while the right ~60% holds a bar chart. The finding headline and key insight statement span the full width above.
- **Full-bleed color data slides**: Slide 10 is a full-bleed composition where the data IS the layout — two large colored rectangles (peach for 2020/39%, lime-green for 2021/47%) function simultaneously as a comparison chart and a visual design element. The data visualization becomes the page layout itself.
- **Generous margins and white space**: Content slides have abundant breathing room. Text never crowds edges.

## Photography & Imagery
- **No photography whatsoever** — this is a purely typographic and chromatic report. No photos, no illustrations, no icons, no decorative graphics. Color blocks and typography do all the visual work.
- **The Artsy logo**: A small "A" mark in a square frame, top-right of the cover. Minimal, architectural.

## Data Visualization
- **Bar charts with color-block styling** (slide 9): Three vertical bars in blue, peach/gold, and pink — each containing a large percentage number (64%, 23%, 13%) with a small label below. The bars are thick, solid-colored rectangles that match the report's block-color language. No gridlines, no axes, no chart chrome.
- **Area-as-data comparison** (slide 10): Two large rectangles (peach = 39%/2020, lime = 47%/2021) fill the entire slide. The relative sizes roughly correspond to the data values. Year labels sit at the bottom. The percentage numbers are enormous. This is data visualization as architecture — the chart IS the slide.
- **No traditional chart apparatus**: No line graphs, no pie charts, no axes, no legends, no gridlines. Data is always expressed through colored shapes and large typographic numbers. The data visualizations are indistinguishable from the decorative color-block system.

## Visual Quirks & Unique Touches
- **Color blocks as both decoration and data**: The same visual language (colored rectangles) serves as the cover art, section dividers, AND data visualizations. This unification is rare and effective — you can't tell where "design" ends and "data" begins.
- **Gallery-wall composition**: The asymmetric color-block grids on divider slides directly evoke a gallery wall hung with colored canvases. For a report about galleries, this is a perfect self-referential design choice.
- **Two-tone section dividers**: The key findings slides (6-7) use bold background colors that change halfway down the page, splitting each slide into two chromatic zones. This is a bold, magazine-editorial move.
- **Headline-as-finding format**: Each key finding is written as a declarative newspaper-style headline — "Online sales keep growing, even as in-person events return." These are designed to be pulled as quotes or social media snippets.
- **Monochrome text discipline**: Despite 7-8 background colors, ALL text is black. No colored text, no white-on-dark reversals (except the very subtle breadcrumbs). This discipline keeps the report legible and lets the color blocks do the emotional work.
- **White gaps as grid lines**: The color-block compositions use white gaps between rectangles rather than actual lines or borders. The negative space creates structure.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Color-as-content**: The broad, art-world-inspired palette of medium-saturation hues is the report's entire visual identity. No photography, no illustration — just color and type. The restraint is radical in its own way: lots of color, but nothing else.
2. **Data visualization = page design**: By making bar charts and comparison graphics from the same colored rectangles used decoratively, the report erases the line between infographic and layout. Data feels native to the design, not inserted into it.
3. **Gallery-wall metaphor made structural**: The asymmetric grids of colored rectangles evoke hung canvases — a perfect visual metaphor for a report about galleries. The design is about its subject without ever being literal.
4. **Headline-first information architecture**: Every finding leads with a bold, quotable statement. The body text is there if you want it, but the key takeaway is impossible to miss. This is optimized for skimming and sharing.
5. **Broad palette, strict rules**: 7-8 colors could easily become chaotic, but the consistent use of black-only text, white backgrounds for reading pages, and uniform block shapes keeps everything cohesive. The system is permissive with color but strict with everything else.
