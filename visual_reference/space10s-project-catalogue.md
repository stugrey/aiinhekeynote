# SPACE10 Project Catalogue — Design Analysis

## Overview
An exhibition catalogue for SPACE10 (IKEA's external research and design lab, based in Copenhagen). It showcases 7 projects available for galleries and exhibitors to borrow/display. The vibe is Scandinavian-minimal meets research-lab warmth — deeply intentional, open-source ethos, sustainability-focused. Feels like a beautifully designed museum handout crossed with a design portfolio. 19 slides total, published 2023.

## Color Palette
- **Black** (#000000) — dominant background for information-dense slides (intro, guidelines, overview, values). Creates a gallery-like void.
- **White** (#FFFFFF) — primary text color on dark slides; background on project detail pages. Clean, bright, clinical.
- **No accent colors** — this is an almost entirely monochrome deck. Color comes exclusively from photography. The restraint is extreme and intentional.
- **Photographic color** — blurred nature imagery provides all the warmth: soft greens, warm ambers, golden bokeh, teal (Tomorrow's Meatball), lush garden greens. The photos do the emotional work that color palettes do in other decks.

## Typography
- **Primary Typeface**: A clean, modern sans-serif throughout (likely a grotesque like Helvetica Neue or similar). Consistent weight, no decorative fonts anywhere.
- **Headings**: Large, light-weight white sans-serif on photographic/blurred backgrounds (slides 1, 2, 7, 16, 18). Feels airy and unhurried.
- **Body Text**: Small, regular-weight sans-serif. Set in neat columns on project pages. Readable but not dominant — the photography leads.
- **Project Titles** (slides 9–15): Bold weight, left-aligned, slightly larger than body. Clear hierarchy: Title → Subtitle → Tags → Body → Links.
- **Section Labels**: Tiny italic text top-left ("Intro", "How it works", "Guidelines", "Overview", "Our values") — consistent wayfinding, very discreet.
- **SPACE10 Wordmark**: Bold, condensed, all-caps — appears bottom-left as a consistent footer on most slides. The closing slide (19) blows it up to fill the entire frame.
- **Page Numbers**: Bottom-right, small — a print-catalogue convention carried into the digital format.

## Layout Patterns
- **Three distinct slide types**:
  1. **Full-bleed photo slides** with overlaid text (slides 1, 2, 5, 7, 16, 18) — atmospheric, blurred photography fills the frame
  2. **Black information slides** (slides 3, 4, 6, 8, 17, 19) — text-heavy, structured, dark background
  3. **White project detail slides** (slides 9–15) — consistent template repeated for each project

- **Project detail template** (the core of the deck): Three-column layout on white:
  - Left column (~20%): Project title, subtitle, category tags in grey
  - Center column (~25%): Body text description, "Learn more here" and "Download assets" links
  - Right column (~55%): Large hero photograph, with a row of 4-5 smaller thumbnail images anchored at the bottom-left

- **Consistent footer**: "SPACE10" bottom-left, page number bottom-right on every page
- **Generous white space** on project pages — content doesn't fill the frame, creating breathing room
- **Blurred photo as texture**: Several slides use heavily blurred, bokeh-style photographs as backgrounds — the blur removes detail and turns photography into pure color/atmosphere

## Photography & Imagery
- **Two photography modes**:
  1. **Blurred/bokeh backgrounds**: Extreme gaussian blur on nature/object photography. Creates dreamy, abstract color fields. Used for section dividers and emotional slides (1, 2, 5, 7, 16, 18). Flowers, plants, and warm natural objects are visible as soft shapes.
  2. **Sharp, editorial project photos**: Crisp, well-lit images of physical installations, prototypes, food, architecture, and exhibition spaces. Professional but not sterile — there's life and warmth in every shot.

- **Thumbnail strips**: Each project page has 4-5 small square/rectangular thumbnails along the bottom showing different angles, materials, and contexts of the project.
- **Subject matter**: Bee homes in meadows, artisan food preparation, wooden architectural models, autonomous vehicle renders, spherical garden structures, reimagined meatballs on teal backgrounds, communal living spaces with greenery. Everything connects to sustainability, nature, and future-living.
- **Circular crops** (slide 17): Three circular portrait/nature photos for the values section (Sustainability, Accessibility, Diversity) — the only non-rectangular image crops in the deck.
- **No illustrations, icons, or graphic elements** — photography carries everything.

## Data Visualization
- **None** — this is a pure catalogue/portfolio format. No charts, graphs, or statistics. The closest thing to data is the structured overview table (slide 8) listing all 7 projects with titles, descriptions, thumbnails, and page numbers — essentially a table of contents.

## Visual Quirks & Unique Touches
- **Extreme blur as a design device**: The blurred photography is the most distinctive element. It transforms documentary photos into painterly, abstract fields of color. It says "this is about feeling, not just information."
- **Connected word-path diagram** (slide 5): "Browse → Download → Exhibit → Share" arranged in a stepped, connected path with right-angle connector lines — a simple process flow overlaid on a blurred background. The words are set at different angles/positions, creating a wayfinding diagram.
- **Underlined keywords** (slide 16): "sustainability, accessibility and diversity" — the underlines are a subtle typographic emphasis in an otherwise decoration-free deck.
- **Print-catalogue conventions in digital**: Page numbers, table of contents with page references, consistent footer, three-column grids — this deck was designed to work as a printed document too.
- **"Hej + welcome"** (slide 2): The Danish greeting immediately establishes place and warmth. The "+" instead of "and" is a small but notable brand voice choice.
- **Open-source language throughout**: "Download assets", "open-source design", "free and open-source" — the design mirrors this openness with its minimalism and accessibility.
- **Category tags in grey** (project pages): Small, light-grey keywords below each project title (e.g., "Parametric Design, Democratic Design, Sustainability, Digital, Fabrication, Open Source") — functional metadata that adds context without visual weight.
- **Final slide — giant wordmark**: Slide 19 is just "SPACE10" in massive bold white text centered on black. A confident, full-stop ending.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Photography-as-color-palette**: By keeping everything else black and white, all color and emotion comes from the photographs. The blurred images especially turn photography into an abstract, atmospheric design tool — color without content.
2. **Rigid template, varied content**: The project detail pages use an identical three-column layout for all 7 projects, but the diverse photography and subject matter prevent monotony. The system creates consistency; the content creates variety.
3. **Print-digital hybrid design**: Page numbers, table of contents, columnar text layouts — this deck respects print conventions while being digital. It would work beautifully as a physical booklet.
4. **Radical chromatic restraint**: No brand colors, no accent hues, no colored backgrounds (besides black). In a world of colorful brand decks, this is striking in its monochrome discipline. The confidence to let photography be the only source of visual warmth is rare.
5. **Scandinavian design ethos made tangible**: The minimalism, the natural materials in the photography, the sustainability messaging, the open-source philosophy — every design choice reinforces the SPACE10 identity without ever being heavy-handed about it.
